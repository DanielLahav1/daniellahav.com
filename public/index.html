About

A short introduction

I am a PhD student in the Department of Physics and the Institute of Nanotechnology and Advanced Materials (BINA) at Bar-Ilan University, working in the group of Prof. Lior Klein. My thesis, Flexible spintronics under strain, examines how mechanical strain reshapes magnetic anisotropy, magnetoresistance, and spin–orbit effects in thin-film devices fabricated on flexible substrates.

Alongside my research I operate the sputtering, lithography, and dicing systems at Bar-Ilan’s nano-fabrication center, where I fabricate the devices I study — and I build much of my own tooling, from low-noise measurement electronics to micromagnetic-simulation and magneto-optical imaging software.

Research

What I work on

Planar Hall effect magnetometry

Elliptical planar Hall effect sensors in soft permalloy films — engineering growth- and shape-induced anisotropy to reach picotesla-scale resolution, extend the usable field range, and understand the anisotropy physics that sets the noise floor.

Flexible spintronics under strain

Spintronic heterostructures fabricated on flexible substrates, using strain as a knob: how bending and stretching modify magnetic anisotropy, anisotropic magnetoresistance, and spin–orbit torques, toward magnetic devices for flexible electronics.

Methods, end to end

Thin-film deposition and device patterning, angular magnetotransport and noise spectroscopy, magneto-optical Kerr effect microscopy, and GPU micromagnetic simulation with MuMax3 — including the control and analysis software behind them.
